Pike County, located in the far eastern corner of Kentucky, is known for its deep Appalachian roots, scenic mountains, and rich coal mining history. As the largest county in Kentucky by land area, Pike County has played an important role in the history and culture of eastern Kentucky. Here are ten interesting facts about Pike County.

1. The Largest County in Kentucky
Pike County is the largest county in Kentucky by land area, covering more than 780 square miles. Its large size means it contains many small communities, mountains, and rural areas throughout the county.

2. Named After a Famous American Explorer
The county was named after Zebulon Pike, the American explorer and soldier who is also the namesake of Pikes Peak in Colorado.

3. Pikeville Is the County Seat
The county seat is Pikeville, which serves as the center of government, healthcare, education, and business in the county. Pikeville has grown into one of the most important cities in eastern Kentucky.

4. Home to the Hatfield–McCoy Feud
One of the most famous family feuds in American history, the Hatfield–McCoy feud, took place partly in Pike County during the late 1800s. The conflict between the Hatfield and McCoy families became legendary and is still associated with the region today.

5. Coal Mining Shaped the Local Economy
For generations, coal mining was a major part of Pike County’s economy. The coal industry helped shape many communities and provided jobs for thousands of families across the county.

6. Breaks Interstate Park Is Nearby
Pike County is located next to Breaks Interstate Park, often called the “Grand Canyon of the South.” The park sits along the Kentucky–Virginia border and offers hiking trails, scenic overlooks, fishing, and outdoor recreation.

7. The Pikeville Cut-Through Project
One of the most impressive engineering projects in Kentucky is the Pikeville Cut-Through Project, which moved nearly 18 million cubic yards of earth to reroute the Levisa Fork River and reduce flooding in downtown Pikeville. It is considered one of the largest earth-moving projects in the Western Hemisphere.

8. Appalachian Culture Runs Deep
Pike County is deeply rooted in Appalachian culture, including traditions such as bluegrass music, storytelling, mountain crafts, and family heritage that has been passed down for generations.

9. Pikeville Is Home to a Medical School
The University of Pikeville is located in the city and includes the Kentucky College of Osteopathic Medicine, one of the leading rural medical schools in the country.

10. A Strong Community Spirit
Despite economic challenges over the years, Pike County is known for its strong sense of community. Residents take pride in their local schools, churches, traditions, and Appalachian heritage.

Quick Facts About Pike County
Established: 1821
County Seat: Pikeville
Region: Eastern Kentucky / Appalachian Mountains
Land Area: ~787 square miles
Known For: Coal mining history, Hatfield–McCoy feud, and Appalachian culture
